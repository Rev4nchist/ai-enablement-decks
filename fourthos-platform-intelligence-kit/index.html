Platform Intelligence
Working Kit

Two working tools — plus a shareable CTO briefing — for extracting and structuring Christian Berthelsen's vision of the Fourth customer-facing AI platform — the system that lets any restaurant's AI agent connect through Fourth's governed front door. Use these together across multiple conversations to progressively build a complete specification from the source.

Tool 01 of 03
Architecture
Canvas
A live diagram of the full platform — every component laid out spatially so you and Christian can point at specific pieces and correct them in real time.
  • Open during calls — the whole architecture is visible without scrolling
  • Click any component to open an annotation panel and capture his exact words
  • Mark each box as Confirmed, Inferred, or Unknown as the conversation moves
  • Export the annotated state as JSON after each session — import it back next call to pick up exactly where you left
  • The progress bar shows how much of the vision is documented vs. still unknown

  • Use alongside the canvas — when a question gets answered, flip to the canvas and mark it confirmed

Confidence key
Confirmed — in his own words from transcript or deck
Inferred — our best read, not yet verified by him
Unknown — open question, needs his answer
Status updates live on the canvas during calls. Export preserves all states.
Operating rule
Source first
Every confirmed item links back to his exact words — the June 4 transcript or the governed AI access deck. Nothing is marked confirmed from inference alone.
Operating rule
Canvas is the spec
The Notion project page for the External Developer Experience Portal gets its content from this canvas. When all boxes are confirmed, the canvas becomes the brief.
Operating rule
Wrong is useful
Inferred boxes exist to be corrected. A wrong guess on the canvas provokes a faster, more specific response than an open question. Use the wrong labels deliberately.
