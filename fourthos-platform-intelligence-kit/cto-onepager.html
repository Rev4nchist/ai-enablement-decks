Fourth is building a governed intelligence platform that lets customers connect their own AI tools — and any AI vendor in their restaurant — directly to Fourth's systems. Scheduling data, labour signals, inventory, back-office. All of it. Through one secure front door that Fourth controls, audits, and charges for.

This is the Salesforce model. A platform other AI systems are built on — not a scheduling app competing with scheduling apps.

How a customer connects their AI to Fourth
1
Customer brings their AI tool. Could be Claude, Copilot, a bespoke scheduling agent, a restaurant analytics product — anything that supports standard AI connections (MCP).
2
They sign in with their own identity. Every request runs under the real user's credentials — not a shared service account. What they can see is what they're permitted to see.
3
The request hits Fourth's governance layer. Fourth controls which tools are available, enforces limits, logs every action, and injects the right location-level context and playbook data.
4
The AI gets a governed, curated answer. Not a data dump — a scoped, contextualised response. If the customer's AI is asking about labour costs, it gets labour cost intelligence shaped by 25 years of Fourth's hospitality-specific knowledge.
5
Fourth has a signed receipt. Every cross-system action is auditable. Customers, IT teams, and head office can prove what their AI did, when, and under whose authority.
What this looks like in practice
"Show me which of my Tier-1 locations are at risk right now — labour trending over budget, support tickets up, and renewal coming in 90 days."
One question from the customer's own AI tool. Fourth's platform pulls scheduling data, support signals, and contract state — each under the right user's permissions, in one audited workflow. The answer arrives as a live, filterable dashboard. Not a spreadsheet. Not a three-day analysis.
Restaurant consultants can use this to track performance across all their client sites. CIOs can use it to get a governance view across 200 locations in a single session. Partner vendors building AI tools for the hospitality space connect to Fourth's intelligence layer rather than building their own.
Fourth stays relevant even when customers use competitors
If a restaurant chain switches scheduling vendors, they still need Fourth's intelligence layer to give that new vendor the right context. Fourth charges for the platform even when it's not the scheduler. The data asset outlasts the application.
The governance layer is the product other vendors can't clone
Any vendor can build an MCP connection. No competitor has 25 years of hospitality-specific operational data, playbooks, and location context. That intelligence, governed and queryable, is what makes Fourth's front door worth connecting to.
CMA is what makes the customer's AI agent actually work
Claude Managed Agents is the runtime that lets a customer's AI tool run a long, multi-step query across Fourth's systems without them building the infrastructure. CMA + Fourth's governance layer = a customer's AI that's actually useful in production.
